CONTRACT MANAGEMENT

A project awarded to a main contractor usually comprise of a number of activities that are specialized and require expertise that may or may not be available with the main contractor. In such circumstances, parts of the whole work is subcontracted to specialized parties that have the resources (expertise, manpower, equipment, sources, etc.) to carry out the work.

In order for a subcontract to be as ideal as possible, it has to have a very carefully developed agreement comprising of scope of work in detail, endorsement from both parties that the scope, responsibilities and liabilities are addressed satisfactorily, terms and conditions drafted as per the standard industry practices such as FIDIC or like. Once the agreement is accepted and signed by both parties, it becomes binding and legal.

If required, this document can be legalized by a competent authority to make it failsafe.
